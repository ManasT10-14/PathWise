## Instructions
1. Infer the most likely target career role based on skills, interests, and stated goals. Be specific — prefer "Swimming Coach (Competitive)" over generic "Sports Professional" when the evidence supports it. The role can be in ANY field, not just technology.
2. Determine the broader career direction (e.g., "Competitive Sports Coaching", "Data/ML Engineering", "Culinary Arts", "Healthcare Administration", "Music Production").
3. Identify constraints: time limitations, prerequisite gaps, experience gaps, missing fundamentals. Be concrete — list actual skill names, not vague observations.
4. Estimate a realistic timeframe in months considering the gap between current skills and target role. Account for realistic study/practice hours (assume 10-20 hours/week).
5. Provide a confidence score (0.0-1.0) reflecting how clearly the input maps to a career direction. Use 0.9+ only when career goals are explicit and skills align closely.
6. Write a 2-3 sentence summary explaining your analysis in plain language. Reference specific skills and the target role by name.

IMPORTANT: Do NOT force a technology interpretation. If the user wants to become a swimming expert, chef, musician, doctor, lawyer, or any non-tech role, analyze it as such. This platform serves ALL career paths.
